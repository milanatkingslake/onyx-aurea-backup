About Cascaded Subscriptions

Cascaded subscriptions automatically extend manual subscriptions to related items, based on subscription rules. For instance, suppose your company's business rules require you to have offline access to an incident’s tasks. Your system administrator can create a subscription rule that automatically subscribes the related tasks to you when you subscribe to an incident.

Where your system administrator has set cascade rules, cascaded subscriptions are also created for any new or updated items that are related to the one to which you had previously subscribed. For instance, suppose the following:

  • There is a subscription rule that ensures that when you subscribe to an incident, you are automatically subscribed to all the tasks of that incident. Subsequently, when a new task is created for that incident, a subscription to that new task is automatically created for you.
  • There is a subscription rule that automatically subscribes a company's primary contact to you when you subscribe to the company. Subsequently, when the primary contact for that company is changed to another individual, a subscription to the company’s new primary contact is automatically created for you. You would, however, need to manually unsubscribe from the previously subscribed primary contact (individual).

Unsubscribing items can affect subsequent cascaded subscriptions. When you unsubscribe from an item, a block is put on that item that limits how it is re-subscribed to you in the future. This is to lessen the likelihood that you will be inadvertently resubscribed to the item. For more details, see Unsubscribing from data.

The previous examples assume cascaded subscription behavior based on subscription relationships and rules that may not be configured on your system. Your system administrator determines the relationships and rules that result in cascaded subscriptions. Contact your system administrator for information about the subscription relationships and rules that affect cascaded subscriptions for your system.

Cascaded unsubscriptions and deletions

Cascaded unsubscriptions and deletions occur as well. For instance, if you subscribe to a company but then subsequently unsubscribe or delete the company, cascaded unsubscriptions or deletions occur for all the incidents owned by that company and all the tasks owned by those incidents. Note, however, that if you were to unsubscribe or delete an incident, while the tasks owned by that incident would be unsubscribed or deleted, the customer that owns that incident would not be unsubscribed or deleted.

The cascade rules that govern this behavior are called required rules. They are based on existing OEP rules that require that certain items belong to and cannot exist without other items. For instance, you cannot create a task without assigning it to an incident, and you cannot create an incident without assigning it to a customer, but you can have an incident without tasks, and you can have a customer without incidents.
