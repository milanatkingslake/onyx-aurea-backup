Territories

In Assignment Administration, creation begins at the Territories level. A territory is an entity that contains conditions. Companies and individuals are associated with a territory if they meet at least one of its conditions. Territories can also contain other territories to provide further organization of the conditions. A geographic example is country, region, state: United States (highest territory), Pacific Northwest (second highest territory), and Washington (third highest territory).

Under each Territories node are condition nodes for individuals, companies, and incidents. Under each condition node are child expressions and linked assignment sets (pools of users). Within the Assignment Tree (navigation area), you can develop the following types of assignment territories:

  • Company territory

A company territory contains one or more company conditions. The first condition that evaluates to true determines which territory a company is associated with.

  • Individual territory

An individual territory contains one or more individual conditions. The first condition that evaluates to true determines which territory an individual is associated with.

  • Incident territory

An incident territory contains one or more incident conditions. The first condition that evaluates to true determines which territory an incident is associated with.

You can create an incident territory when your business rules for routing incidents do not require properties of the company or individual objects.

  • Combined territory

A combined territory contains one or more incident conditions and one or more customer conditions. For company or individual records, the first condition that evaluates to true determines which territory they are associated with.

Incident conditions in a combined territory are only evaluated if the incident's owner is also associated with the territory. The first condition that evaluates to true determines which territory the incident is associated with.

You can create a combined territory when your business rules for routing incidents require properties of the company, individual, and incident objects.

  • Container territory

A container territory holds other territories without conditions.

Conditions

Conditions contain expressions or logical statements. Expressions can use any property of the company, individual, and incident objects combined with operators and values. Example expression: countryCode = US. The expressions in each condition refer to the properties of the related object. For example, the expressions in an individual condition refer to the individual object.

A territory can have any number of conditions and they are used for:

  • Associating companies, individuals, and incidents with territories.
  • Creating internal contacts for individuals, companies, and incidents.
  • Assigning incidents to OEP users.

Evaluating conditions

The Assignment Studio evaluates conditions in order, from the top to the bottom of the Assignment Tree (navigation area). Through this sequence of evaluation, a company, individual, or incident record is associated with the first condition that evaluates to true, even when lower conditions might also evaluate to true.

When a condition evaluates to true, customer and incident records are associated with the appropriate internal contacts, and incidents are reassigned to the appropriate internal contacts, customers, and territories. Processing runs in the background and is not visible to end users.

You can reorder processing of a territory's contained territories, conditions, and expressions by using the and arrows on the toolbar to reposition items. For example, you could reposition a high-priority condition, such as month long campaign at the top of the list of conditions to ensure first priority for processing.

For more information about condition processing, see About processing conditions.

Organizing territories

The name "territory" implies a geographic assignment; however, you can organize territories by other classifications, such as market sector, named accounts, and much more. Your assignment structure should match your organization's business model for assigning customers and incidents. For planning information on planning territories, see Assignment Studio.
