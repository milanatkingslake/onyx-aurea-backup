About contact groups

Contact groups organize contacts into two different groups: Customer and Internal. When contacts are associated with records, they are assigned to one of these two contact groups.

  • Customer

Customer contacts are used to show relationships between customer records in the database and can be used to model the customer’s organization. Customer contacts apply to individuals or companies with whom you do business (your customers). A good example of a customer contact relationship is showing an individual as an employee of a company. Additionally, subsidiaries, partners, and other company records can be linked to a main company record to display the structure of the company.

  • Internal

Internal contacts are individuals within your organization appointed to manage the customer. Internal contacts are used to show relationships between customer records and employees (users) within your organization. An example of an internal contact relationship is showing one of your sales representatives as the designated sales representative for one of your customers. Internal contacts are user names of your employees that are defined with the Security Administration tool.

There are parent/child relationships between the Customer contact groups and Internal contact groups which are the parent records, and the contact types that you create, which are the child records. Contact types created in the Customer group are used to model your customer’s organization. Contact Types created in the Internal group are used to model your organization.

By creating these contact relationships, users can readily view these relationships and access other records and users which are connected in some way to the record of interest.

Contacts are found on the following types of records: Company, Incident, Individual, Customer Product, and Work Tickets.
