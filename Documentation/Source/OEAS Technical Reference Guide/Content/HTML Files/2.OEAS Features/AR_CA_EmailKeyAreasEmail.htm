Key areas of the Email feature

Key areas of the Email feature

The key areas of the Email feature are:

  • Attachments for emails
  • Contact associations
  • Non-contact associations
  • Merged templates
  • The email monitor subsystem
  • Administration of the Email feature

Attachments for emails

With the Email feature, users can attach files (word processing documents, spreadsheets, sound files, and snapshots) from their hard drive to email messages. Users can also add attachments to their email messages from existing files and literature items in OEAS. For more information, see Attachments for email messages.

Contact associations

The email system records the contact associations (links) to internal users, individuals, and/or companies that have sent or received an email. Names or partial email addresses are resolved against internal users and individuals in the user interface to create contact associations. For more information, see the Email business objects in the Business Objects Library.

Non-contact associations

The email system also records the non-contact associations (links) to individual, company, and incident records that are unrelated to the sender or receiver of the email. Using non-contact associations, you can see a conversation between two users related to a customer on that customer’s history list. For more information, see the Email business objects in the Business Objects Library.

Merged templates

Merged templates enable users to insert into the body of an email message pre-written text and key pieces of data, such as work-ticket or customer summary information. Merged templates require the use of method calls, which you create in Method Call Designer, an OES design tool. For more information, see Merged Templates.

The email monitor subsystem

The Onyx Processing Server (OPS) email monitor subsystems (EMSs) monitor configured mailboxes on network mail servers at set intervals for inbound mail. EMSs process and save emails into the database.

You can configure an EMS to:

  • Monitor a corporate email account, such as a generic sales, service, or support inbox.
  • Monitor an email account set up in conjunction with Onyx Email for Outlook inbox. Onyx Email for Outlook is a plug-in for Microsoft Outlook with which end-users can save messages into the database from their personal Outlook inboxes and track new messages sent from their personal account in OEP. For more information, see Onyx Email for Outlook.

An EMS can also:

  • Create contact associations based on the email addresses of the sender and recipients.
  • Create non-contact associations based on the previous email in the thread.
  • Flag new emails for follow-up so they can be reviewed by internal users.
EMS Limitations:
  • Unicode and accented characters are not supported for EMS. EMS does not deliver, store, or retrieve mail from mailboxes or folders with that contain Unicode or accented characters. To ensure email message delivery, be sure that folder names use the ASCII character set.
  • EMS does not support Transport-Neutral Encapsulation Format (MS-TNEF or TNEF) or rich-text format (RTF) for email messages. When an OEP user opens a message that was sent in one of these formats, any TNEF- or RTF-formatted text in the message body does not appear.

For more information, see Email monitor subsystems.

Administration of the Email feature

The domain data to support the Email feature company-wide is configured using the administration tools of the OES. Administration opportunities include the following:

  • Configuring the EMS in OPS
  • Configuring attachments for emails
  • Configuring email address book item entries (queues)
  • Configuring relative dates to enhance date searches for emails
  • Configuring template groups and creating method calls for merged templates

For detailed information about the configuration options for the Email feature, see the material on configuring Email and on configuring templates.
