Elements of a survey

The main elements of a survey are:

  • Question

A question is a prompt whose purpose is to obtain information from respondents. A question is not associated with a survey.

  • Survey question

A survey question is an instance of a question that is associated with a survey.

  • Response

A response is a possible answer for a question. A response is not associated with a question.

  • Question response

A question response is an instance of a response that is associated with a survey question.

You can make a response editable. When a respondent selects an editable response, he or she can add text to the response.

  • Question hierarchy

A question hierarchy is a structure that links question responses to subsequent survey questions, thus determining which survey questions are asked and in what order.

Creating a question hierarchy is optional; if you don’t create one, the survey questions are presented according to their list order in the survey in Survey Administration.

  • Answer

An answer is a respondent’s composition or choice of response (or responses) for a survey question.

For free-text questions and editable responses, the respondent can compose his or her own text. An example of an editable response is, “Other,” to which a respondent to add particulars.

In addition to free-text questions, there are multiple-type and single-type questions. For answers to single-type questions, the respondent can only select one question response. For multiple-type questions, the respondent can select more than one question response.

When a respondent completes a survey, the answers are associated with that respondent. As a result:

  • If the respondent takes that survey again, those answers overwrite the answers chosen or written the first time he or she took the survey.
  • If the respondent takes two surveys that each have a survey question that is an instance of the same question, the respondent’s answer to the survey question in the second survey overwrites his or her answer to the survey question in the first survey.
