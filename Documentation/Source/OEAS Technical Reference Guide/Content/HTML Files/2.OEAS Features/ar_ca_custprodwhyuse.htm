Why use customer products?

A typical use of a customer product record is to track and save details of a customer’s purchase.

Example: Ticket holders for an Off-Broadway show

Several performances have been cancelled due to plumbing problems in an old Off-Broadway theatre. Ticket holders must be notified about the cancellation and given new performance dates.

The sales associate for the theatre performs a query to identify all ticket holders for the cancelled performances. The sales associate then locates each ticket holder’s customer product record, which contains details of the purchase including the performance date, ticket price, and other relevant information about the ticket holder. Having the purchase information in one central location enables the sales associate to quickly contact the ticket holder, inform them of changes, and update their record with the new performance date. Optionally, the sales associate can include a literature product such as a form letter to notify the customer of future performances.
