Subscribers

Subscribers are OEP end-users who can subscribe to the records that they want to copy to OEP-Offline. To enable an OEP end-user the ability to subscribe to records, you must create that end-user a subscription profile for OEP-Offline. Once you have created a subscription profile for the end-user, he or she can subscribe to records.

Before you can create subscription profiles for OEP end-users, you must define a staging server and configure your synchronization subsystem. For more information, see the OEP-Offline Implementation Guide.

The subscription profile also enables you to:

  • Turn synchronization on or off for the user.
  • Turn subscription on or off for the user.
  • Reset the user’s existing offline data.

For more information about creating subscription profiles, see Managing subscription profiles.

In addition to enabling OEP end-users the ability to subscribe to records themselves, you can create advanced subscribers—OEP end-users who can subscribe to records on behalf of one or more offline users. For instance, suppose that your account services director assigns new accounts (customers) to his team of account managers. You can make your director an advanced subscriber so that as he assigns the new customers, he can also subscribe those customers to the managers to whom they belong. The next time the managers synchronize their offline data, they automatically retrieve their new accounts and can access them while they are disconnected from the network.

The general rules for advanced subscribers include that:

  • An advanced subscriber can subscribe subscribable data to any number of offline users.
  • An offline user can have any number of advanced subscribers subscribe to data on their behalf.

For more information about creating an advanced subscriber, see Creating a proxy subscriber.
