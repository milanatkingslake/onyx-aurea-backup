Why use work notes?

Among the many uses of work notes, two of the most common are:

  • To facilitate discussion of the issues in a task, incident, or work ticket
  • To provide important information to the person responsible for completing or resolving a task, incident, or work ticket

The design of the Work Notes feature, which lets users add new work notes successively rather than modify existing work notes, adds an inherent chronology to the work notes and preserves the integrity of existing work notes.

Example: Supplementary sales information

Suppose a company, GlobalHospice, operates hospices associated with hospitals. GlobalHospice is negotiating a contract with a hospital chain for providing post-operative recovery care. In order to track the progress of the negotiations, GlobalHospice’s lead negotiator has attached work notes to the sales incident for the contract. Suppose now that a GlobalHospice employee, John Taylor, has been assigned to research similar contract negotiations. John attaches another work note to enter issues that his research has raised. Another GlobalHospice employee, Carol Sampson, opens the incident, reviews John’s work note, and adds a work note containing her analysis of the issues that John has raised. When the hospital chain suspends negotiations because of a change in key personnel, GlobalHospice’s lead contract negotiator adds another work note summarizing the state of negotiations at that point. Suppose then that Carol is assigned the lead-negotiator duties. As she reviews the work notes attached to the sales incident, she has before her:

  • The original lead negotiator’s progress notes.
  • John’s research results.
  • Her own analysis of the issues raised by John’s research.
  • A summary of the state of negotiations when she took over as lead negotiator.

As this example shows, when someone is assigned to pursue a sales opportunity, work notes provide that person with an ongoing context and chronology of events to date regarding the opportunity.
