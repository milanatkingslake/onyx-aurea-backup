About processing conditions

Conditions contain expressions (logical statements) that are processed by rules. The following example shows the United States territory, its Washington territory, the HighTech condition, and the HighTechGroup assignment set.

Contained in the HighTech condition are the following expressions:

  • countryCode = US
  • regionCode = WA
  • marketSector = 1002

The rule is: If countryCode = US and regionCode = WA and marketSector = 1002 then create the assignments for the HighTechGroup. If the condition evaluates to true (that is, if all of its expressions evaluate to true), UserA is assigned to the incident.

Evaluating conditions

The Assignment Studio evaluates conditions in order, from the top to the bottom of the Assignment Tree (navigation area). Through this sequence of evaluation, a company, individual, or incident record is associated with the first condition that evaluates to true, even when lower conditions might also evaluate to true.

When a condition evaluates to true, customer and incident records are associated with the appropriate internal contacts, and incidents are reassigned to the appropriate internal contacts, customers, and territories. Processing runs in the background and is not visible to end users.

Unlike the company and individual update process, the incident update process is not configured to run on every incident update. For incidents, the update process only runs when an OEP user manually re-assigns an incident to a particular user called the Auto Assign user. For more information, see Manual assignment of incidents.

You can reorder processing of a territory's contained territories, conditions, and expressions by using the and arrows on the toolbar to reposition items. For example, you could reposition a high-priority condition, such as month long campaign at the top of the list of conditions to ensure first priority for processing.

Assignment Studio business objects

Assignment Studio business objects are responsible for providing information to the OPM processes about internal contacts, incident assignments and their related conditions.

About processing conditions

The following end user workflow describes how conditions are evaluated and processed through OEAS and OPM:

  • The OEP user creates a company, individual, or incident record and saves it to the Onyx database.
  • At run time, the OPM process retrieves an ordered, filtered list of expressions (grouped by condition) from OEAS.
  • The OPM process finds the first condition that is true for the record and stops evaluating the expressions. (A condition is true when all of its expressions are true.)
  • OPM associates new and updated records with the territory that owns the condition.
  • OPM assigns the incident and creates the correct internal contact records.

Condition processing runs in the background and is not visible to end users.
