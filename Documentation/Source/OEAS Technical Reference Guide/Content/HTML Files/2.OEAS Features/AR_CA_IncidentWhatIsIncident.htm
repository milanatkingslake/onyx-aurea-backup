Incidents

Incidents provide the primary means for tracking activities associated with customers (companies and individuals). These activities can include sales opportunities, service requests, support requests, and possibly others. The three default incident categories, organized around these activities, are:

  • Sales incidents
  • Service incidents
  • Support incidents

With the Onyx Enterprise Application Server (OEAS), you can configure this feature to include other categories of incident activities that are important to your company. For further information on configuring additional incident categories, see “Configuring OEAS features” in Customizing OEAS.

Sales incidents - Sales incidents are used to categorize and assign sales opportunities. With sales incidents you can keep unlimited work notes, manage quotes and proposals, manage the sales pipeline, forecast sales, schedule appointments, and assign tasks.

Service incidents - Service incidents are used to categorize, assign, and track service-related customer issues. With service incidents you can keep unlimited work notes, track issue resolution, and categorize and assign tasks.

Support incidents - Support incidents are used to categorize, assign, and track support-related customer issues. With support incidents you can keep unlimited work notes, track resolution, and categorize and assign tasks.

Functionally, the Incidents feature enables end-users to assign tasks and keep work notes as a means of tracking customer issues. Users can relate incidents to incident products, forecasts and quotes, tasks, reminders, and tags. In addition, users can access an incident audit log to view the history of activity for a particular customer interaction.

The main components of the Incidents feature include the following:

  • Incidents
  • Work notes
  • Audit logs

Incidents: The incident includes a brief description of the activity or information that it documents, as well as its source, status, priority and severity levels, and resolution codes. It contains information regarding who is currently assigned to the issue, the amount of time expended on the issue, tags associated with the issue, and so on.

Work notes: The work notes of an incident are the text notes entered by the end users that provide information about the incident’s activity or issue. When an end-user creates a work note, OEAS records the date it was created and the ID of the user who created it, enabling other users to quickly identify who provided that particular information on the issue. Users can perform periodic saves on a work note that they are actively writing. However, once a user has saved and closed the work note, it cannot be updated and for any additional comments they want to add, the user must create another work note. Work notes are designed in this manner to ensure the integrity of the historical data.

Audit logs: The audit logs are dated entries that OEAS creates automatically when an incident is created and each time it is updated. It contains key details of the incident, including the current source, status, priority, severity, and assigned-to details. End users can review the audit-log entries for changes in incident details that might not be noted by the end user in the work note, including who changed the priority of a incident and when that change was made. Audit logs cannot be updated or deleted once they have been created.

Characteristics of an incident:

  • Is owned by a customer (company or individual)
  • Must have an incident category
  • Can have any number of audited entries, work notes, tasks, tags, file attachments, and associated work tickets
  • Can have only one reminder and one alert
  • Can be associated with only one incident product
  • Can be assigned to only one user
  • Can be associated with any number of users as internal contacts and external contacts
  • Can have a single primary contact, which is either an individual or a company.
