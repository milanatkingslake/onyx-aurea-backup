Reorganizing territories

Business rules change and evolve over time, and in the Assignment Studio this development translates into changes within the structure of the Assignment Tree or the logic of the expressions that define conditions within the tree. To support evolving businesses, the Assignment Studio offers a way to update the company or individual associations within a territory using List Manager.

List Manager is a tool used for bulk processing of OEP records and for generating lists of customers, and you can use this tool for maintaining territories. In List Manager, you can:

  • Query the customer's assigned territory.
  • Re-assign companies and individuals to new territories.
  • Retire a territory, either by deleting its customer data or by moving the data into a new territory.

The following example reorganizes a geographic territory by re-assigning companies and individuals to a new territory. For further instructions on List Manager, see the OEP Help and "FAQ" in the Assignment Administration Help.

Example: Reorganizing a geographic territory

A Western sales region in the United States needs to put the state of California into its own sales territory. Customers with California addresses must be re-assigned into the new territory.

To make these changes, the administrator:

  • Creates a new California territory separate from the Western sales region in the Assignment Tree. New customers with addresses in California are associated with this territory.
  • Re-assigns all companies and individuals with California addresses currently associated with the Western sales region to the new sales territory. To accomplish this task, the administrator chooses all companies with a California address and then runs the "Reassign Territory" bulk action in List Manager. When the bulk action is complete, the administrator repeats this procedure with individuals.

Considerations

When you reorganize territories, consider the following:

  • The “Reassign Territory” bulk action only supports the update of company records or individual records (not a combination of both).
  • Re-assignment of customers is required. For more information, see Creating incident territories.
