Creating incident territories

For the routing of incidents, you can define the following types of incident territories:

  • Incident only territory
  • Combined territory
Incident only territory

Incident territories contain one to many incident conditions and zero customer conditions. The first condition that evaluates to true determines which territory an incident is associated with. You can create an incident territory when your business rules for routing incidents do not include properties of the company or individual objects.

For example, a sales representative requires the ability to track incoming incidents on a daily basis. The business rules do not include customer data for the tracking of incidents. The administrator creates an incident territory and enables only the incident insert process. The incident update process is not used.

The new incident territory does the following:

  • Associates incident records with the appropriate territory
  • Creates internal contacts for incidents
  • Assigns incidents

During the insert of an incident record, the system evaluates the conditions to see if any internal contacts should be created for the incident or if the incident should be assigned to a particular user.

Combined territory

A combined territory contains one to many incident conditions and one to many company/individual conditions. Incident conditions in a combined territory are only evaluated if the incident's owner is also associated with the territory. The first condition that evaluates to true determines which territory the record is associated with. You can create a combined territory when your business rules for routing incidents include properties of the company or individual object in addition to the incident object.

For example, a support representative is assigned to resolve incoming support incidents whose company owners are in the High Tech sector. The business rules iarar_ca_anclude customer and incident data. The administrator creates a combined territory to do the following:

  • Associate individual, company, and incident records with the appropriate territories created for the High Tech sector.
  • Create internal contacts for customers and incidents.
  • Assign incidents to support engineers in the High Tech sector.

During the insert of individual, company, and incident records, the system evaluates the conditions to see if the inserted record should be associated with the designated territories. The system also creates the correct internal contact records and for incident records, assigns incidents to the appropriate support representatives.

Considerations

When you define incident territories, consider the following:

  • Incident territories are evaluated by the system according to the hierarchy defined in the Assignment Tree. A high-priority incident condition, such as an important sales opportunity will be processed before other incident conditions if it evaluates to true and is at the top of the hierarchy.
  • OPM processes are used for the insert and update of incidents, companies, and individuals. You must enable these steps to call whatever processes are appropriate for your business.
  • Combined territories require that an incident's owning customer record be associated with the same territory.
