Using surveys: an example

Suppose the following:

  • Global Technologies sells two software products, Product A and Product B.
  • Jane Thompson, a marketing executive at Global Technologies, needs customer self-profiles in order to differentiate customers by:
  • Which of products A and B they use
  • How they use them and how much benefit they realize using these products
  • The markets and world segments that they target
  • Based on what Jane learns from the customer self-profiles, she will customize marketing campaigns for each group.

Jane could use the Survey Administration tool in Onyx Enterprise Studio to create a survey with the following question hierarchies.

In the following diagram, diamond shapes are questions, rectangles are responses, and ovals are editable responses.

Jane takes the following steps to create this survey (for information on performing these tasks, see Help for Survey Administration in the OEAS Administrator’s Reference):

  • Create a survey type named “Self-profile.”
  • In the “Self-profile” survey type, create a survey named “Region 3 customers.”
  • In the survey, create the following survey questions, question responses, and question hierarchy:
  • Survey question: Which Global Technologies product do you use more actively?
    Question responses: Product A, Product B
    Question type: Single
    This survey question’s responses start a pair of question hierarchies, one for each answer.
  • Survey question: Where do you use Product A?
    Question responses: Centrally, In multiple locations
    Question type: Single
    Whichever question response the respondent picks, the next survey question in the hierarchy is the same: “What features would you like to see added to Product A?”
  • Survey question: Who mostly uses Product B?
    Question responses: Managers, Line workers
    Question type: Single
    Whichever question response the respondent picks, the next survey question in the hierarchy is the same: “What features would you like to see added to Product B?”
  • Survey question: What features would you like to see added to Product A?
    Question responses: Feature #1, Feature #2, Other features. The question response “Other features” is editable.
    Question type: Multiple
    However the respondent answers, the next survey question in the hierarchy is the same: “In which markets do you use Product A?”
  • Survey question: What features would you like to see added to Product B?
    Question responses: Feature #1, Feature #2, Other features. The question response “Other features” is editable.
    Question type: Multiple
    However the respondent answers, the next survey question in the hierarchy is the same: “In what world segments do you use Product B?”
  • Survey question: In which markets do you use Product A?
    Question responses: Direct, Channel, Both
    Question type: Single
    Whichever question response the respondent picks, the next survey question in the hierarchy is the same: “How much do you save monthly with Product A?”
  • Survey question: In what world segments do you use Product B?
    Question responses: Western Hemisphere; Europe, Middle East, Africa; Asia Pacific
    Question type: Multiple
    Whichever question response the respondent picks, the next survey question in the hierarchy is the same: “How much do you save monthly with Product B?”
  • Survey question: How much do you save monthly with Product A?
    Question responses: Less than $10,000; $10,000-$50,000; More than $50,000
    Question type: Single
    This is the last survey question in one branch of the question hierarchy.
  • Question: How much do you save monthly with Product B?
    Question responses: Less than $10,000; $10,000-$50,000; More than $50,000
    Question type: Single
    This is the last survey question in one branch of the question hierarchy.

After Jane completes the survey, she:

  • Obtains customer responses with a client application (OEP, OCC, or a custom application).
  • Uses the List Manager feature of OEP to group respondents by:
  • Product usage
  • Market
  • Monthly sales
  • Compiles the answers to product-feature requests as captured by the answers to the free-text “Desired features?” survey question.
