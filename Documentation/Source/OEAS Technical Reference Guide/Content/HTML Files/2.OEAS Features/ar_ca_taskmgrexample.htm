Using Task Manager: an example

Suppose that Roland, the lead for an account team at Global Financial Services, has just returned from vacation. To prepare for his team’s status meeting, he opens Task Manager and runs some filters that he created before going on vacation. The purpose of these filters is to view only the tasks that are currently relevant to his team.

“Status” - Roland uses the Status filter to select the following:

  • Sales incidents of the Project and Relationship Management groups
  • Tasks of both Open and Closed status

To prepare, Roland runs this Status filter with all his team members selected. To group each team member’s tasks, he sorts the resulting list by his team member’s names.

“Accomplishments” - To zero in on what his team has completed while he was gone, Roland created the Accomplishments filter by modifying the Status filter to select the following:

  • Sales incidents with Status of Finished and Closed
  • Tasks with Status of Closed and Closed Pending Confirmation

“Hot List” - To highlight developments that came up while he was gone, the Hot List filter specifies tasks and incidents created between the beginning and ending dates of his vacation.

In order to continue using the Hot List filter, Roland changes the filter’s dates from the fixed dates of his vacation to “7 days before today.”

At the meeting, as Roland gets status from his team members, he performs batch updates to various groups of the sales incidents and tasks on which his team members report progress.
