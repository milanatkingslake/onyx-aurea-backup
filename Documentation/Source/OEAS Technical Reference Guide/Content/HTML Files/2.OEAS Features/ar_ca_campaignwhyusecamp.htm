Why use campaigns?

A typical use of a campaign is to track a related group of marketing activities.

Example: Campaign as a tracking tool for a marketing activity

Suppose that you want to create a marketing campaign to encourage your customers to use your online banking service. You call this campaign Online Banking Promotion.

You decide to advertise your online banking service in five different ways (these are your tracking codes): with an advertisement placed on your company's Web site; with an advertisement placed on your partner company's Web site; with an advertisement in the local weekly paper; with an advertisement placed in the local Sunday paper; and with a promotional postcard sent to your current valued customers in the mail.

After you determine how to advertise your online banking service, you logically arrange the events into three groups (your tracking code types):

  • Internet Ads: The advertisements being placed on your company and partner company's Web sites.
  • Newspaper Ads: The advertisements in the local weekly and Sunday newspaper.
  • Direct Mailer: The promotional postcard (it does not fall into the same category as the advertisements, so it gets it own category).

Remember that these tracking code types are independent of your Online Banking Promotion campaign. After you create these tracking code types, you can associate any number of tracking codes with them, and you can associate them with any number of campaigns. Additionally, you can associate tracking code milestones with a tracking code to manage the specific actions required to meet the goals of the campaign.

For example, if your company were to offer a Free Checking Promotion, you could use these same tracking code types again, but the specific tracking codes and their associated tracking code milestones must be different.

The campaign feature enables you to monitor the effectiveness of your Online Banking Promotion campaign at any time. When a customer subscribes to your online banking service, not only is their customer data entered into the database, but so is the tracking code—that is, the name of the advertisement that encouraged them to use your online banking service. By running a report based on your campaign, you can measure the effectiveness of each of your marketing activities (tracking code types), and the overall effectiveness of your campaign. You might discover that Internet Advertising was the most effective marketing activity and that the Direct Mailer was the least successful. With this information, you will be better equipped to launch your next promotion.
