What is a customer product?

When a product is associated with a customer record it becomes a customer product record. A user creates a customer product by selecting a product to associate with a customer record (company or individual), as shown in the following example.

A user can also create customer product records using literature products. Literature products are special types of standard products that include functional attributes not included with standard products, such as the ability to be sent directly to a printer. However, in the context of the Customer Products feature, these additional functional attributes of the literature product are ignored.

The types of products that can be associated with a customer product record include:

Standard products - The products users can associate with a customer product, literature order, or forecast/quote record. Some examples include health or auto insurance, corporate training, storage space, software upgrades, tickets to a seminar or sporting event.

Literature products - The special types of standard products that include functional attributes not included with a standard product, such as the ability to be sent directly to a printer. Some examples include marketing collateral, such as letters, brochures, pamphlets, posters, press materials, white papers, direct mail items, and photographs; saleable items, such as mailing lists, software programs, or printed documentation; or informational packets or promotional pamphlets that you send to customers to promote and supplement your standard products.

For details on administering these two types of products, see Product Administration Help.
