Forecasts and Quotes

A forecast is a document that provides the means to assign a probability to anticipated sales. The forecast contains a price total weighted by both the confidence that a customer will purchase a product(s) and the probability of closing the entire sale.

A quote is a standard business document that provides a business client with an estimate of the price for a product(s) or service(s).

Each forecast can have several lines of detail as can each quote. Each forecast line has an associated confidence level for the product under consideration (a likelihood of the sale for that item) in that line.

Forecasts and quotes are typically associated with a sales incident, although you can also use them with support and service incidents. Each incident can have only one forecast associated with it, but an incident can have multiple quotes associated with it.
