Contacts

The Contacts feature provides users a means of establishing and maintaining relationships between records in the database (customer, incident, task, work ticket, and customer product) and internal users and customers (both individuals and companies). Contacts are categorized by groups, categories, and types.

For information on primary contacts, see Customers.

Contact groups

Contacts are organized by contact groups: customer and internal. Each contact group can be associated with many contact types.

Customer - Customer contacts define relationships between companies and individuals with whom you do business. An example of a customer contact is a purchasing manager at an external company. An end user can set this person as a customer contact on that company’s main record so that all internal employees are aware of this information.

Although customer contacts are usually used to establish contact relationships at the customer's organization, users can assign any individual or company (in the system as a customer record) as the external contact for an incident, task, or customer product, as well as any other customer record.

Internal - Internal contacts define a relationship between a record and a user in the database. Users can add one or more internal contacts to each record. Internal contacts are generally individuals within your organization appointed to manage the customer. An example of an internal contact relationship is specifying a sales representatives as the designated sales representative for a customer. Each Onyx user added using Security Administration is an internal contact.

Contact categories

Contacts are further organized by category, with each category existing in both contact groups (customer and internal). You can assign multiple internal and external contacts to each category. Each contact category can be associated with many contact types. The contact categories for the Contacts feature are:

  • Company - This contact category supports the internal and customer contacts on the Company form of the OEP client.
  • Incident - This contact category supports the Contacts tab for internal and customer contacts on the Incident form of the OEP client.
  • Individual - This contact category supports the internal and customer contacts on the Individual form of the OEP client.
  • Product - This contact category supports the internal and customer contacts for customer product records, as available on the OEP client on the Products node of the Companies or Individuals PowerPage.
  • WorkTicket - This contact category supports the internal and customer contacts for work ticket records.

Contact types

Contact types classify contacts within the contact group/category combinations. You define contact types using Contact Type Administration, creating logical groupings of contacts for a contact group/category combination. Each contact type must have a contact group and a contact category.

Contact types created in the customer group of a contact category are used to model your customer’s organization. Contact types created in the internal group of a contact category are used to model your organization.
