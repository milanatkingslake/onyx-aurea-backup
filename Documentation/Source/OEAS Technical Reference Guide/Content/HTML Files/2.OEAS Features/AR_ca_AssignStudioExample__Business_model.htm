Sample business model

This sample business model provides an overview of the common configurations of territories, conditions, expressions and assignment sets in the Assignment Studio.

Who are we?

Global Widget, Inc. is a west coast business that sells Widgets and the super Giga-Widget to other companies. Sales opportunities are gathered from a number of sources and entered by people unfamiliar with the details of the companies’ sales organization. However, Global Widget requires face-to-face contact with their customers in order to make an effective sales pitch.

The company has two regional sales offices: one in Seattle, Washington and the other in Sacramento, California. Each regional sales office includes an account manager and a senior sales executive as well as a team of sales people.

How do we sell?

The sales people all work for commissions. For fairness, new leads are distributed to the pool of sales people using the Round Robin assignment type. There is a complication -- only the senior sales executive is allowed to work with sales opportunities for the rare but valuable Giga-Widget product. The regional account manager is the internal contact for each company and sales incident.

After the sale, support steps in

Global Widget also provides its customers with a support organization. The support organization consists of two teams: Team A is the front-line personnel and Team B is the senior support engineers. Team A has the ability to escalate incidents to Team B (senior-level engineers), when they are unable to solve the issue in a timely manner.

Choosing processes

After the territory assignment structure is defined, the administrator enables OPM processes for the evaluation of the assignment conditions. For Global Widget, processes are required for the insert and update of incidents and companies.

Step 1: The administrator considers how company or individual records will be inserted or updated by employees.

  • Global Widget, Inc. is a business-to-business operation. Sales people interact with companies but not individuals. Employees create and update customer records on a daily basis.

Best practice: The business rules do not include individual data. A company territory is defined and the company insert process is enabled.

Step 2: The administrator considers how incidents are tracked and assigned to leads.

  • Global Widget requires the ability to track incoming incidents on a daily basis.

Best practice: The rules do not include customers. An incident territory is defined and the incident insert process is enabled. Since the rules for handling incidents do not include customer properties, there is no need to enable the customer update process. Only combined territories will require the customer update process.

  • When support engineers are unable to resolve incidents in a timely manner, they need the ability to re-assign incidents to senior-level support engineers. In this case, incidents are re-assigned to the Auto Assign user. The senior-level support engineers are created as an assignment set for the Auto Assign user in Assignment Administration. For more information, see the Manual assignment of incidents.

Best practice: The rules include only incident data, however, the escalation occurs when an incident is updated. Territories are defined and the incident insert and update processes are enabled.
