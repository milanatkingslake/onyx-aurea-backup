Applying OED customizations

When you save your customizations to the Onyx Enterprise Dictionary (OED), the Object Designer saves your changes to the working copy, not the production copy, of the OED. This means that you can make a series of customizations over time, even incomplete customizations, without affecting the production copy of the OED.

To apply your changes to the production copy of the OED, you must publish your changes. When you publish your changes, the Object Designer overwrites the production copy of the OED with the contents from the working copy. This means that the next time that the OED cache is refreshed (when the COM+ application is restarted), your changes will be reflected in the OED cache. Users of OEAS can implement your changes immediately.

Important: You can make multiple modifications before saving your work. However, if the save fails for any reason, the entire action fails and no changes are saved. Therefore, Onyx recommends that you save your work frequently to avoid data loss due to browser interruptions or other system failures.

When the Object Designer saves your changes to the OED, a partial validation is performed on your work which includes validating the modifications with the Onyx XML schema. However, cross-dependencies are not checked. For more information on the validation process, see validation.
