Working with email monitor subsystems and monitored mailboxes

Email monitor subsystems (EMSs) monitor configured mailboxes on network mail servers at set intervals for inbound mail. EMSs process and assimilate inbound emails into the database. Using information such as the sender and recipient email addresses and an internal thread identifier, the EMS associates inbound email with companies, individuals, Onyx users, and customer records.

Note: EMS does not support Transport-Neutral Encapsulation Format (MS-TNEF or TNEF) or rich-text format (RTF) for email messages. When an OEP user opens a message that was sent in one of these formats, any TNEF- or RTF-formatted text in the message body does not appear.

You can configure an EMS to:

  • Monitor a corporate email account, such as a generic sales, service, or support inbox.
  • Monitor an email account set up in conjunction with the Onyx Email for Outlook inbox. Onyx Email for Outlook is a plug-in for Microsoft Outlook with which end-users can save messages into the database from their personal Outlook inboxes and track new messages sent from their personal account in OEP. For more information, see Adding and configuring a mailbox for Onyx Email for Outlook.

An EMS can also:

  • Create contact associations (links) to individual, company, and incident records related to an email.
  • Create non-contact associations (links) to emails associated with individual, company, and incident records unrelated to the sender or receiver of the email.
  • Flag new emails for follow-up so they can be reviewed by internal users.
